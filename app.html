Circulatory System

Back

The circulatory system is a complex network that plays a crucial role in sustaining life. It consists of the heart, blood vessels, and blood, working together to move blood throughout the body. This system ensures that oxygen and essential nutrients are delivered to cells and tissues, supporting their function and vitality. At the same time, it removes metabolic waste products, such as carbon dioxide, maintaining a stable internal environment.

There are two primary circuits within the circulatory system: the systemic and the pulmonary circuits. The systemic circuit delivers oxygenated blood from the heart to the rest of the body and returns deoxygenated blood back to the heart. The pulmonary circuit transports deoxygenated blood from the heart to the lungs, where it releases carbon dioxide and picks up oxygen before returning to the heart. Blood vessels, including arteries, veins, and capillaries, form an extensive network to transport blood and facilitate the exchange of oxygen, nutrients, and waste products.
