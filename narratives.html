Systems

Circulatory system

The circulatory system moves blood, delivering oxygen and nutrients while removing waste.
Digestive system

The digestive system breaks down food into nutrients and eliminates waste.
Immune system

The immune system defends against infections and diseases by identifying and destroying harmful invaders.
Integumentary system

The integumentary system includes the skin, hair, and nails, providing protection and helping regulate temperature.
Muscular system

The muscular system enables movement and maintains posture through muscle contractions.
Nervous system

The nervous system controls and coordinates activities by transmitting signals to and from different parts of the body.
Respiratory system

The respiratory system facilitates breathing, taking in oxygen and expelling carbon dioxide.
Skeletal system

The skeletal system provides structure, protects organs, and supports movement.
Urinary system

The urinary system removes waste from the blood and regulates water and electrolyte balance.

Types

Bacterium

A bacterium is a single-celled microorganism that can exist either as an independent organism or as a parasite.
Cell

A cell is the basic building block of all living organisms, capable of independent function and reproduction.
Organ

An organ is a group of tissues that work together to perform a specific function in the body.

Techniques

Litography

Lithography is a printing technique that uses a flat surface treated so that ink adheres only to the design to be printed.
Scanning Electron Microscopy (SEM)

SEM is a technique that uses electrons to create detailed images of the surfaces of materials at high magnifications.
SEM colorized

Colorized SEM involves adding color to SEM images to highlight specific features or for aesthetic purposes.
Transmission Electron Microscopy (TEM)

Transmission Electron Microscopy (TEM) uses a beam of electrons passing through an ultra-thin specimen to create detailed images of its internal structure.
